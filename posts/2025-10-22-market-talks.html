market talks

last sunday i went to a supermarket with my friend d so we could buy something to drink. i bought a can of tonic and he bought a can of monster. there we talked with some people. i, then, decided write about these interactions.

the hopeless

we were commenting about how stuff was so much more expensive, when a worker agreed with us. then the three of us started talking:

"so, do you like working in this supermarket?" - d said.

"i love it so much that i'm going to quit this job tomorrow." - the worker replied, with a clear sarcasm.

"and what do you plan to do after that?" - i asked.

"drugs i guess." - he replied casually. - "what about you guys? what do you plan to do?"

"international relations or linguistics." - i replied.

"no idea." - said d.

"how are you gonna make a living though?" - i asked.

"i won't." - he replied with a smile.

the saleswoman

after the previous talk, we went on through the market, until we heard a female voice.

“hey, boys!” - the saleswoman said, calling us from behind a stand with lots of instant noodle cups and some bowls. - “would you like to try this new flavor of cup noodles?”

“i’m actually not a fan of these noodles.” - i replied - “they arenot very healthy, you know?”

“yea… i must agree with you about this.” - she replied me, with an awkard expression.

“i wanna try it!” - d said, a bit excited. - “hmmm… it’s actually better than i expected.”

“thanks a lot!” - she said, as if she were a chef who made a wonderful meal. - “they are, indeed, very tasty.”

“by the way, if you buy two cup noodles of the same flavor, you get this nice bowl.” - she said while grabbing a branded red bowl.

“sorry, i don’t have enough…” - d replied.

the cashier

as we paid, the cashier and us talked a bit:

“hello, guys. are you paying together?”

“hello.” - we replied. - “we aren’t”.

“ah… the sunset must be happening right now…” - he said.

“you wish you could see it?” - i asked.

“yea. but i’m stuck in this damn supermarket.”

“this job must suck, huh.” - d said.

“it surely does. you guys must study to don’t end up like me.”
